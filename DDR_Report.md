### 1. Property Issue Summary

The Master Bedroom at the Sample Site exhibits two distinct issues: significant cracking on the east wall, which appears to be related to structural settling, and considerable thermal leakage around the window, indicating poor insulation and potential air infiltration. These issues impact both the structural integrity and energy efficiency/comfort of the property.

#### Master Bedroom

*   **East Wall Cracking:**
    Significant cracking is observed on the east wall of the Master Bedroom. This visual evidence suggests an underlying issue of structural settling.

*   **Window Thermal Leakage:**
    The Master Bedroom window displays significant thermal leakage, identified by distinct blue signatures in the thermal imaging. A temperature differential of 5 degrees Celsius was detected at the window seal, confirming poor insulation and possible air infiltration.

### 3. Probable Root Cause

*   **Wall Cracking:** The cracking on the east wall is most likely caused by structural settling of the building.
*   **Window Thermal Leakage:** The thermal leakage at the window seal is attributed to inadequate insulation and potential gaps allowing air infiltration.

### 4. Severity Assessment

**Moderate to High.**

*   The "significant cracking" on the east wall, identified as potential "structural settling," indicates a potentially serious issue that could compromise the building's integrity if not addressed. Structural issues can worsen over time and require professional intervention.
*   The "significant thermal leakage" with a 5-degree Celsius temperature differential at the window seal represents a considerable energy loss, leading to increased heating/cooling costs and reduced indoor comfort. While not immediately structural, it significantly impacts the property's habitability and operational expenses.

### 5. Recommended Actions

1.  **Structural Engineer Consultation:** It is highly recommended to engage a qualified structural engineer to conduct a thorough assessment of the cracking on the Master Bedroom's east wall. This will determine the precise cause, extent, and necessary remediation steps for the structural settling.
2.  **Window Insulation and Sealing:** Address the thermal leakage at the Master Bedroom window. This may involve re-sealing the window frame, improving insulation around the window, or considering a replacement of the window unit if the existing unit is found to be defective or beyond repair.

### 6. Additional Notes

*   It is advisable to monitor the wall cracks for any changes in size or progression until a professional structural assessment can be performed.
